## Academic Papers and Research

### Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence
- Pfeifer, R., & Bongard, J. (2006). *How the Body Shapes the Way We Think: A New View of Intelligence* - Foundational text on embodied cognition
- Pfeifer, R., Lungarella, M., & Iida, F. (2007). "Self-organization, embodiment, and biologically inspired robotics" - Science journal article
- Brooks, R. A. (1991). "Intelligence without representation" - Classic paper on behavior-based robotics
- Brooks, R. A. (1999). "Cambrian Intelligence: The Early History of the New AI" - Collection of influential papers

### Humanoid Robotics
- Kajita, S., et al. (2010). *Honda humanoid robot - its planning and control* - Early Honda humanoid development
- Kuffner, J., et al. (2008). "Action languages for robotics in the Semantic Robot Description Language" - Robot description and control
- Asfour, T., et al. (2008). "The humanoid robot ARMAR-III: Implementation of human-like behaviors in anthropomorphic robots" - ARMAR humanoid platform
- Metta, G., et al. (2008). "The iCub humanoid robot: an open platform for research in embodied cognition" - iCub open platform

### Sim-to-Real Transfer
- Tobin, J., et al. (2017). "Domain randomization for transferring deep neural networks from simulation to the real world" - Domain randomization techniques
- Sadeghi, F., Levine, S. (2017). "CADRL: Learning to collide using reinforcement learning" - Learning collision avoidance
- James, S., et al. (2019). "Sim-to-real via sim-to-sim: Data-efficient robotic grasping via randomized-to-canonical adaptation networks" - Advanced sim-to-real techniques
- Peng, X. B., et al. (2018). "Sim-to-real transfer of robotic control with dynamics randomization" - Dynamics randomization for control

### Vision-Language-Action Integration
- Chen, X., et al. (2021). "An empirical study of training end-to-end vision-and-language transformers" - Vision-language models
- Radford, A., et al. (2021). "Learning transferable visual models from natural language supervision" - CLIP model paper
- Brown, T., et al. (2020). "Language models are few-shot learners" - GPT-3 capabilities
- Alayrac, J. B., et al. (2022). "Flamingo: a visual language model for few-shot learning" - Multimodal language models
